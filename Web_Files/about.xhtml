The Federalist Papers: An Analysis

About

Do you ever question whether politicians say what they truly believe in? We generally see politicians today as persuasive orators with selfish motives to make their audience believe something that is not truthful or real seem real and attainable. Our research question for this project is the following: How do elites such as Alexander Hamilton in the Federalist Papers apply their political ideologies to appeal to those of opposing ideologies of the lower and populist political ideals?

This is a digital humanities project aimed to analyze the rhetoric of Alexander Hamilton in the Federalist Papers to see if it reflected his own political ideals or appealed to the masses who opposed the British Crown.
